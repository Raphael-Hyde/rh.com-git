Methods for Facilitating Pet Adoption

Discovery / Research / Design / Next Steps
How might we

Match pets with suitable owners?

Millions of animals are currently in shelters and foster homes awaiting adoption. How might we help an adopter find a pet which matches their lifestyle, considering factors including breed, gender, age, temperament, and health status?
The challenge
According to the ASPCA® each year, approximately 7.6 million animals enter animal shelters. Adoption clinics often allow anyone to adopt, creating dangerous environments for the animal and owner.
Proposed Solution
With the use of Google's Tensorflow, Assistant, and Chatbase Pet-Prints a web based pet pairing platform creates personality profiles to map pet compatibility to potential owners faster with more accuracy.
Potential Impact
Stronger and more compatible pet relationships, improved quality of life for those in need, and reduction in animal executions. These outcomes would likely reduce the number of abandoned animals overall improving the lives for patients, vets, the elderly, and more.

Key research takeaways

Is this the right one?

Many pet owners felt that they knew which animal was the right match yet ended up being overwhelmed with the care and training required.

Pet Finding Apps / Stats

60% or 9/15 did not use apps or sites to locate their pets.

53% said they wouldn’t change anything about the process.

Improper Matching

Most people don’t ask what dogs they should have, rescues say they want to find the right adapter, but they really don’t have the time.

Use machine learning to study the positioning of animals, see if certain patterns increase adoption frequency. Similar to shelf placement in a store, certain locations are more attractive than others.

Indecent Owners

Use machine learning to prevent people from adopting when they don't have a conducive lifestyle or if they have a history of bad ownership.

Competitor Evaluation

Key Findings

Old school Tools

Most of the leading tools and websites are in need of a modern refresh. Although pet matching apps exist, they're dated.

Centralized Data

The industry is in need of a decentralized knowledge base where 3rd parties can connect and facilitate the process with update to date listings.

No personalized matching

Some sites ask basic questions to make recommendations, however deep compatibility matching doesn't appear to be accessible.

Wireframing Core Loops

Pet Archetypes & PET AND PATIENT PAIRING
Project Hypothesis
By gathering user data via chatbots the frequency of trust building may
increase, creating more comprehensive matches.

Step 1: Designing the Chatbot flow

Through designing the chatbot core loop I was able to surface questions which I answered with a flow chart of onboarding questions. By using Chatfuel (chatbot engine) I was able to experiment with different dialogs and inputs. I would like to explore the use of Google's Chatbase for analytics, and Allo + Assistant for communication.

Step 2: Chatfuel Experiment

Learning's from prototype
My tests concluded the following are completed pre-dev. Define user input rules, configure database (ideally through firebase), and validate data sharing thresholds. Are users comfortable with a digital agents? What is the proper duration for data collection?

Step 3: Designing Match Results View

Once the chatbot collects the user's preference data and assembles a personality profile (via Google Tensorflow) the user will be presented with a collection of compatible matches based on animal archetypes and if applicable medical benefits.

Step 4: Pet Profile View

Pet profiles will display their archetypes, compatibility measures, and potential conflicts based on profile matrix.

Step 5: Profile Comparison View

Once an adopter selects pets from their search they have the option to compare archetypes and profile matrices.

Step 6: Schedule pet visit

After learned about pet profile compatibility the adapter can contact the shelter or rescue to schedule a visit.

Next Steps
